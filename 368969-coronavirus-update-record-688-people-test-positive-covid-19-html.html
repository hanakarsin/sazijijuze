COVID-19: Kenya confirms record 688 new cases, national tally rises to 12,750

April 2024 · 3 minute read

- Health CS Mutahi Kagwe said 4,522 samples were tested in the last 24 hours with 425 positive cases being males while 263 were females

- The youngest was a seven-month infant while the oldest was 95-years-old

- On a positive note, 457 more patients recovered but three more succumbed to the disease

The coronavirus situation in Kenya appears to be getting worse day by day as more people get infected with the deadly virus.

Health CS Mutahi Kagwe on Saturday, July 18, a record breaking confirmed 688 more COVID-19 cases in the last 24 hours, raising the national tally to 12,750.

The CS was speaking in Embu county on Saturday afternoon during the daily briefing on the pandemic.

"This disease is here with us and is ravaging our people in all the counties. To control the spread of the virus calls for personal responsibility. I want to propose another slogan..you save me..I save you. Take the responsibility of saving your parents, your friend and any other person," said Kagwe.

The senior government official said 4,522 samples were tested in the last 24 hours with 425 positive cases being males while 263 were females.

The youngest was a seven-month infant while the oldest was 95-years-old.

He noted three more patients had died from the disease but 457 more recovered on a positive note.

"Four hundred and fifty seven people have recovered from the disease raising number of recoveries so far to 4,440. Out of the 457 new recoveries, 401 were under home-based care while 56 have been discharged from hospitals. Three more patients succumb to COVID-19, fatalities now at 225,"he said.

Kagwe lashed out at politicians once again for holding public gatherings despite the ban imposed by President Uhuru Kenyatta.

"I want to warn that people should be aware and shun political meetings because if you get sick you won't see them. Politicians have caused infections and denied, so If you don’t go to political meetings they will remain wherever they are won’t call meetings, they do call those meetings because they know you are foolish enough to attend,"he said.

Globally, over 14.2 million had contracted the respiratory illness as Saturday, July 18, afternoon.

Out of these, 8.4 million had recovered while 600,000 had succumbed to the virus.
