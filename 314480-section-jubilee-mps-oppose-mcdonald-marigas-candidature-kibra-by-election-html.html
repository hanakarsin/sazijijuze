Section of Jubilee MPs oppose McDonald Mariga's candidature for Kibra by-election

June 2024 · 3 minute read

- Jubilee party settled on McDonald Mariga after an intensive interview for the ticket

- Section of MPs opposed his candidature with many saying DP Ruto was imposing the footballer

- They vowed to support ODM candidate in protest over Mariga's nomination

- Another aspirant has written to party secretary general Raphael Tuju protesting the Jubilee party choice

A section of Jubilee MPs have opposed McDonald Mariga's candidature in the Kibra by-election saying the party was imposing the aspirant on the voters.

The MPs vowed to campaign for the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) candidate to protest their party's choice.

Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria and nominated legislator Maina Kamanda were in the forefront to publicly oppose Mariga's candidature and promised to support the ODM nominee.

Kamanda said he would not allow Deputy President William Ruto to ruin the peace brought by the handshake between Raila Odinga and President Uhuru Kenyatta with his “selfish political interests”.

“The candidate Jubilee Party picked has been imposed on the party by Ruto. He is Ruto’s candidate and not Jubilee’s. I am for a candidate who will be announced by ODM,” said the nominated MP.

On his side, Kuria said Kibra needed a candidate who understands the area better and according to him, Bernard Musungu was best suited to serve the people.

He said should ODM party settle on Musungu, he will be in the forefront to back his candidature for the November 7 by-election.

Nairobi Senator Johnson Sakaja who had earlier on asked the party to back off from the unnecessary competition in the mini poll was non committal on who to support.

He instead reiterated the people of Kibra needed a good leader to continue the legacy of late MP Ken Okoth.

“I had asked my party to back a candidate from ODM, but that request was declined. What I can say is that Kibra needs a good leader who can continue what Ken started,” said Sakaja.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, another aspirant who lost the Jubilee party ticket to Mariga, Morris Kinyanjui, has written to party secretary general Raphael Tuju protesting the former's nomination.

Kinyanjui argued the nomination was not only unfair and deceptive but also shrouded in secrecy.

“To make matters worse, Mariga is not a resident or a voter in Kibra constituency,” he argued.

Mariga defeated 16 other aspirants to secure the ticket.
