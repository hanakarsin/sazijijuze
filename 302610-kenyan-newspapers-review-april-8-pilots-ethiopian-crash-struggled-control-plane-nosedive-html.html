Newspapers on Monday, April 8, reported on reactions by various leaders on ODM's loss in Ugenya and Embakasi South by-elections.

The dailies further delved in the 2022 succession politics where DP William Ruto has chosen to turn the heat on Jubilee Party allied leaders who are opposed to his bid to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta.

1. Standard

Preliminary investigations in the Ethiopian Airlines plane crash indicate that pilots struggled to control the aircraft.

According to the Standard, the plane's autopilot was disengaged at 08.39:55 on the morning of Sunday, March 10, shortly after take off.

The plane then started flying in a nose down position forcing the pilots to pull up but with no success.

At 08.41:32, on board computers informed the pilots that they were flying close to terrain and also speeding before disaster struck at 08.44.00.

2. People Daily

The daily reported that Uhuru's appointee for the position of Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai is set to take oath of office.

Chief Justice David Maraga will administer the oath at the Supreme Court on Monday, April 8.

Mutyambai's appointment was given nod by both the National Assembly and Senate where the appointing committee tasked him to explain how he will deal with extra judicial killings.

He will succeed Joseph Boinnet whose term ended in March 2019 but was immediately appointed as Chief Administrative Secretary in the Tourism docket.

3. Nation

The Nation laid bare sharp reactions from various leaders on the shame that befell the ODM Party during Embakasi South and Ugenya by-elections.

According to ANC Secretary General Barrack Muluka, the development should sound a warning bell to the Raila Odinga led party that there is a new sunrise in Kenyan politics.

On her part, Wiper Party Secretary General Judy Sijeny said ODM would not have suffered such humiliation if its leadership listened to a request by Kalonzo Musyoka not to field a candidate in the spirit of handshake.

Orange Party Chairman John Mbadi, however, maintained that the handshake only affected presidential elections and not parliamentary.

4. Star

At least 10 legislators opposed to Ruto's 2022 presidential bid have pressed upon President Uhuru Kenyatta to ensure leaders linked to graft are arrested and prosecuted.

Led by Tiaty MP William Kamket, the Team Kieleweke said they will marshal citizens to arrest corrupt leaders if the president was not willing to flex his muscle.

The leaders sharply differed with Uhuru's approach that the law must be used to bring culprits to book.

They charged that even those who have been mentioned in fraud broke the law by bleeding away public coffers.

5. Taifa Leo

Kalonzo led Wiper Party has expressed displeasure on a decision by allies of Ruto to take credit over party's victory in Embakasi South by-election.

Wiper Party Secretary General Judy Sijeny critisised Ruto allies equating them as people who want to reap where they did not cast a grain.

Taifa Leo also reported that Senator Kipchumba Murkomen on Sunday, April 7, claimed that David Ochieng's victory in Ugenya by elections was a win for the DP.

Murkomen's sentiments were echoed by Kimilili MP Didmus Barasa, Kericho Senator Aaron Churuiyot and Kapseret MP Oscar Sudi.
