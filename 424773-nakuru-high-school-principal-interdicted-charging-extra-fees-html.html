The Teachers Service Commission (TSC) has interdicted Nakuru Boys High School Principal Mike Yator Kiplagat.

In a letter dated Wednesday, September 1, Yator was interdicted after he allegedly charged extra school fees contrary to the Basic Education Act Section 29.

''TSC directs me to say that it is alleged that you have breached Clause b (vi) of the Third Schedule of the Act that you charged extra levies to students in the years 2020 and 2021 contrary to the Basic Education Act Section 29 while you were the Principal Nakuru Boys High School,'' the letter reads in part.

Invited to make defence

Through the letter, TSC Nakuru county Director Fredrick Ng'ang'a said that Yator's interdiction takes effect from Wednesday, September 1, 2021.

According to Ng'ang'a, before the Commission proceeds to consider and determine Yator's case, he has been invited to make a defence statement/response to the Commission in writing.

''This should be written 21 days from the date of this letter. You will be allowed to be heard by the Commission in person. Indicate the nearest TSC sub-county office where you will be required to report once a month,'' Ng'ang'a said.

Directed to present any other evidence

Yator has further been directed to present any other evidence, including documents in support of his case.

''Vacate office and continue to be away. Provide your contact address, email and telephone number and also forfeit your annual leave,'' said Ng'ang'a through the letter copied to TSC secretary and TSC Nakuru East sub-county.

Chavakali High School on the spot

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, a Chavakali High School student who was sent home for lack of school fees has given a heartbreaking account of how he walked 35 kilometres to Kakamega, where he spent the night in the freezing cold.

Linus Kiprop, a Form Two student who comes from West Pokot, was sent away from the school on Wednesday, September 1, and due to lack of fare, he had to walk all the way to Kakamega Town.

"I study at Chavakali High School and I had been sent away for fees. So I walked from the school to Kakamega because I did not have fare. So I was walking and taking occasional rests," he said.

George Magoha's stern warning

As TUKO.co.ke reported, Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha warned school heads against sending home students from needy families.
