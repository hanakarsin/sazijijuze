Ndindi Nyoro: Wanted Kiharu MP dodges police dragnet after hours of standoff at TV station

April 2024 · 3 minute read

- The lawmaker refused to leave the station after receiving information police were waiting outside

- A number of MPs allied to Tanga Tanga went to the media house to stand in solidarity with him

- He was a wanted man following an incident in Murang’a where he and nominated MP Kamanda caused chaos inside a church

- The vocal MP was also accused of assaulting a police officer and resisting arrest

Embattled Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro finally left Royal Media Services (RMS) offices in Kilimani, Nairrobi where he was taking refuge to avoid arrest after getting wind police were waiting for him outside.

The legislator who had on Sunday, September 8, gone to the RMS owned Inooro TV for a talk show treated the audience to a rare reality show when he refused to exit the station fearing he was going to be apprehended.

Confirming the midnight escape, Kandara MP Alice Wahome who showed up to stand in solidarity with a terrified Nyoro, thanked RMS and the people of Murang'a for offering their unsolicited support to the legislator.

"The hopeless attempt by Kibicho and IG to arrest Hon Ndindi Nyoro has failed badly .Thanks to Citizen fraternity, Royal Media , the people of Murang’a , Kenyans using social media who offered overwhelming support. Down with dictatorship," she tweeted.

Nyoro reportedly managed to sneak out of the media house at around 11pm after hours of standoff.

The fiery MP, a staunch member of the Tanga Tanga movement and close ally of Deputy President William Ruto, became a marked man following chaos that erupted in Murang’a on Sunday where he and nominated MP Maina Kamanda clashed in church over protocol.

The legislator was also accused of resisting arrest and assaulting a police officer.

Wahome who accompanied the under siege Nyoro termed the impending arrest an act of impunity arguing arresting the MP at night was against ethics and urged police to write to the Speaker of the National Assembly to warrant a proper arrest.

“The police are the ones supposed to notify arrest. This is impunity happening outside the media," she said."We must challenge the Inspector General not to allow the police to be used to persecute politicians. Politicians should be allowed to support who they want. We will present Nyoro to any police station in the daytime," Kikuyu MP Kimani Ichungwa added.
