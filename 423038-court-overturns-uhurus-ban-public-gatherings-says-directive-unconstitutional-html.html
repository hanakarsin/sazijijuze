Court Overturns Uhuru's Ban on Public Gatherings, Says Directive is Unconstitutional

July 2024 Â· 3 minute read
  • The High Court quashed President Uhuru's ban on public gatherings terming it unconstitutional
  • Justice Anthony Mrima prohibited IG Hillary Mutyambai and his officers from enforcing the directives issued in October 2020
  • Additionally, the judge noted that NSAC is not a state organ and is not a creation of any statute

The High Court has overturned President Uhuru Kenyatta's ban on public gatherings terming it as unlawful and unconstitutional.

Uhuru's directives issued by the National Security Advisory Committee (NSAC) in October 2020, outlawed public gatherings and gave various rules that people holding public gatherings should follow.

In his ruling on Wednesday, August 18, Justice Anthony Mrima said for starters, the National Police Service should not enforce the protocols issued adding the directives on how law enforcement officers should discharge their duties is unconstitutional.

He thus prohibited Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai and his officers from implementing NSAC's directives on public gatherings.

"I have carefully scrutinised the statement issued by the NSAC and ratified by the Cabinet. The statement is conspicuous that it directed the law enforcement officers (who is the National Police Service) to investigate particular offence or offences and also to enforce the law against the particular person or persons," the judge said.

Further, Mrima ruled NSAC is not a state organ and is not a creation of any statute.

According to NSAC's directives, persons seeking to hold public gatherings should notify the Officer Commanding Station (OCS) at least three days before the convention and no more than 14 days before the proposed date.

The Law Society of Kenya had filed a petition against the issued protocols arguing they were an infringement on Kenyans' right to assemble.

In its petition, LSK said the directives were being used to discriminatively and selectively suppress divergent political opinions.

Extension of curfew

As reported on Wednesday, the head of state announced that the nationwide 10pm to 4am curfew will run for another 60 days to curb the spread of COVID-19.

The president also banned public gatherings and meetings, including political rallies and campaign meetings for impending by-elections for another 60 days.

"All public gatherings and political rallies are extended for another 60 days to tame the infection rate of COVID-19 which is ravaging the country," read the statement.

At the same time, the president relaxed the COVID-19 restriction in the zoned counties, citing Kisumu, Migori, Homa Bay, Siaya, Vihiga, Busia, Bungoma and Trans Nzoia, among other counties.
