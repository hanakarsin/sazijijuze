West Pokot Governor Lonyangapuo Says County Will Allocate KSh 700m to Bursaries: &quot; Watahamia Huku&quot;

  • In 2021 alone, the West Pokot county government allocated KSh 700 million towards the bursary kitty
  • Lonyangapuo spoke on Friday, August 6, when he laid a foundation stone for constructing a new administration block at the Chewoyet National School
  • In July 2021, Lonyangapuo quit the Kenya National African Union (KANU) party on whose ticket he was elected governor in 2017

Outspoken West Pokot Governor John Lonyangapuo has asked parents from other counties to relocate to his county for them to enjoy bursaries.

The first-time county boss said that in 2021 alone, his administration had allocated KSh 700 million towards the bursary kitty.

Every student benefitted from KSh 20,000

Lonyangapuo said every student from Form One to Form Four received KSh 20,000.

He took a jibe at his predecessors, accusing them of celebrating after allocating KSh 30 million to the same kitty during their tenure in office.

''They celebrated that they had disbursed bursary to students in Form One to Form Four across the entire county, only KSh 30 million. They shamelessly even celebrated about it,'' Lonyangapuo said.

The county chief asked parents in other counties to relocate to West Pokot and cast their votes to benefit from the bursaries courtesy of his administration.

''It is not all about disbursing the funds anyhow to my own children in this county, but for students from other counties of Trans Nzoia and Turkana, among others, kindly ask your parents to relocate here so that they can as well enjoy the bursaries,'' Lonyangapuo explained.

Chewoyet National School

On Friday, August 6, he spoke when he laid a foundation stone for constructing a new administration block at the Chewoyet National School.

''I have also presented the school with a KSh 2million cheque through the county's schools' infrastructure development fund to support the project,'' said Lonyangapuo.

Lonyangapuo said that 87 students from Form Two to Form Four in the school are vulnerable.

''Because of this, I want to allocate KSh 10,000 to each of the 87 students. And to those in Form three and Four who have acquired their National Identity Cards must vote for me as I seek a second term in office,'' said Lonyangapuo.

Lonyangapuo announces his exit from the KANU party

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, Lonyangapuo announced quitting the Kenya National African Union (KANU) party whose ticket he was elected governor in 2017.

Lonyangapuo said the independence party had used the county assembly members to frustrate his leadership.

He said he would come up with another party which he will use to defend his seat in the next year's vote.

"Don't worry. If I did not lose the seat while vying on a dead party like KANU, how will I fail with my own?" posed Lonyangapuo while addressing a gathering in Makutano.

As he fondly refers to himself, the professor of applied mathematics was the only governor elected on the KANU ticket.

Lonyangapuo's exit has dealt KANU a blow as its leader, Gideon Moi, prepares to give shots in national politics.
