Bandit Terrorists, Boko Haram/ISWAP Are Threats To Our Democracy

August 2024 · 2 minute read

Former President Goodluck Jonathan has lamented the continuous rising cases of insecurity.

The former Nigerian President noted that activities of bandit terrorists, insurgents and other criminals pose a big danger to the country’s democracy.

Naija News reports that Jonathan stated this while speaking in Friday at the public presentation of a research report entitled ‘Terrorism and Banditry: The Nexus’, published by the Goodluck Jonathan Foundation (GJF).

According to the former president, peace is the bridge that links poverty to prosperity.

The former president admitted that from experience nation building can be challenging.

He, however, pointed out that to achieve peace the country has to be secured.

Jonathan said: “Our pursuit of peace cannot be achieved in isolation of the pursuit of justice and other human security needs. This is so because peace is the bridge that links poverty to prosperity, reconciles hope with despair and imposes order on chaos.

“There is no doubt that our nation is plagued by many crises and these challenges have continued to threaten our fate and shared destinies.

“We have lost our loved ones, lost millions of properties and investments because of insecurity. The challenges posed by banditry, terrorism and other associated crimes threaten our human essence and the essential values of our democracy and nationhood.

“This is why the GJF considered it apt to undertake this research with a view to engaging the relevant stakeholders towards improved security.

“As a leader, I have been privileged to preside over the affairs of this nation for five years. I appreciate the challenges that come with nation building and the burden associated with pursuing peace and building trust in times of crises.”
