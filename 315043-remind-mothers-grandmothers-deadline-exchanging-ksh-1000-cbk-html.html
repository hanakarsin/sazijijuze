Remind your mothers, grandmothers deadline for exchanging old KSh 1000 is near - CBK

February 2024 · 2 minute read

Central Bank of Kenya (CBK), the banking sector regulator, has once again reminded Kenyans about the deadline for exchanging old KSh 1000 notes with new ones.

This time CBK also urged members of the public to remind the mothers and grandmothers of the looming deadline, lest they be left with a hoard of old banknotes stashed under the mattress and which will soon be rendered worthless.

The regulator noted they recently received photo showing a bundle of old KSh 1000 notes from a lady who intimated she visited her mother and discovered she had been stashing the money in her secret savings account at home, except she did not know the old notes would soon be out of circulation.

"Someone sent us this picture (shown below), which she took after she visited her mother. Our mothers and grandmothers are epic savers. They may have some money around their homes. Please check on them and remind them of the deadline of September 30 for exchanging the older KSh 1000 notes," CBK tweeted.

The Central Bank had in a related statement advised employers who pay salaries and wages in cash to start paying their employees using the new KSh 1,000 notes.

The regulator reminded the employers their workers will not be able to use the old KSh 1,000 cash past September 30, 2019, the deadline for withdrawal of the old currency from circulation.

The old KSh 1000 notes will be replaced with the new generation currency. All Kenyans have until end of September 2019 to exchange the notes, after which the new ones will fully take over.
