President Uhuru Kenyatta’s rejection of the names of six judges nominated for appointment to superior courts has once again put the Judiciary in the spotlight over integrity issues bedevilling the institution.

While no detailed reasons were publicly shared, the government has maintained that the rejected judges were adversely mentioned in intelligence reports and that Kenyatta was not ready to appoint judges with questionable character.

The recent development will no doubt deepen the trust crisis facing the judiciary; a critical institution that has hogged the limelight for all the wrong reasons in the recent past.

The critical third arm of government has in recent years found itself in murky waters as top judicial officers are increasingly linked to capital offences and other serious crimes amid claims of unbridled corruption in the corridors of justice, further sapping the already low public confidence in the institution.

Below, we take a look at some of the charges and allegations against judicial officers in the recent past.

1. Pauline Chesang

Senior Principal Magistrate Pauline Chesang was in March 2019 arrested and charged in relation to the murder of her lawyer husband Robert Chesang, who was shot seven times in their Machakos home.

Chesang, three police officers and a driver spent three months in police cells before they were released on a KSh 500,000 cash bail. The case is ongoing.

2. Philomena Mwilu

The current Deputy Chief Justice is facing an early exit from office after DPP Noordin Haji filed a damning petition at the JSC seeking her removal. The damning petition lays out a litany of charges touching on gross misconduct, misbehaviour and breach of the Judiciary code of conduct that Haji believes offer sufficient ground to have Mwilu kicked out.

The petition was filed after a five-judge bench blocked an attempt to charge Justice Mwilu in court in relation to alleged criminal conduct. The DCJ faced 13 graft counts in 2018 but moved to the High Court to challenge her criminal prosecution. The court ruled that evidence against her had been obtained illegally but made it clear that the charges against her were factually and legally sound for purposes of commencing criminal proceedings.

3. Justice Sankale ole Kantai

The Appeal Court Judge was arrested in February last year over links to the murder of Dutch businessman Tob Cohen. He is reported to have been a longtime family and business lawyer for the Cohens since the 1990s. The Judge was accused of not only giving Cohen's widow, Sarah Wairimu - who is the main suspect in the killing - advice but also helping her to craft reports to the police.

Wairimu was charged with the murder in 2019 after Cohen’s body was found in an empty water tank in their home compound. She is said to have reached out to the judge hoping to get some advice in case she was caught or implicated in the case.

4. Edgar Kagoni

The Mombasa Principal Magistrate was in late 2019 arrested and charged over the loss of heroin exhibit worth KSh 30 million in a case he presided over. He was, alongside three others, charged with obstruction with intent to defeat justice and aiding and abetting trafficking in narcotic drugs.

The prosecution stated that he mishandled exhibits in the trial of Hussein Masoud Eid Bakari and caused the loss of the seized heroin after he failed to make a determination on an oral application by an investigator, a Corporal Mjomba, who had sought to place the exhibits under police custody. The charges against the magistrate were later quashed by Justice Reuben Nyakundi

Appointment of appellate judges

According to a gazette notice, the appointment of the Court of Appeal of judges was in line with Article 166 (1) (b) of the Constitution of Kenya.

Out of the 40 judges that the Judicial Service Commission (JSC) had recommended to the head of state only 34 were appointed.

Out of the 34, Uhuru appointed nine to the Employment and Labour Relations Court while 18 others were appointed as judges of the Environment and Land Court.

However, Uhuru rejected George Odunga, Joel Ngugi, Aggrey Muchelule, Weldon Korir, Makori Evans Kiago, and Judith Omange Cheruiyot citing integrity issues.
