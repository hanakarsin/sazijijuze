Dollar To Naira Exchange Rate/Black Market Rate

April 2024 · 2 minute read

Friday, 28th January 2022: This is the news about the Dollar to Naira rate at the official and black market exchange rate.

Read Naija News update on the official dollar rates as well as Black Market rates, Bureau De Change (BDC) rates, and CBN rates.

The official rate today, Friday, January 28th, 2022, for $1 dollar to naira = ₦415.42/$1.

According to the data at the FMDQ Security Exchange where forex is traded officially, the exchange rate between the naira and the US dollar opened at ₦415.42/$1 on Friday 28th, after it closed at ₦416.33 to a $1 on Thursday, 27th January 2022.

Naija News reports that the dollar to naira exchange rate has maintained an average of N416.69 to a dollar since the beginning of the new year.

How much is a dollar to naira today in the black market?

The exchange rate for a dollar to naira at Lagos Parallel Market (Black Market) players buy a dollar for N567 and sell at N570 on Friday, January 28th, 2022, according to sources at Bureau De Change (BDC).

Please note that the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) does not recognize the parallel market (black market), as it has directed individuals who want to engage in Forex to approach their respective banks.

Trading at the official NAFEX window

The exchange rate between the naira and the US dollar opened at ₦415.42/$1 on 28th January 2022 and closed at ₦416.00/$1. Showing a change of -0.08%.

According to data from FMDQ, forex daily turnover stands at $81.29 million.

Spot rate: The dollar sold to the naira as high as ₦444.00 and as low as ₦405.00

A spot exchange rate is the current price level in the market to directly exchange one currency for another, for delivery on the earliest possible value date.

Meanwhile, the Nigerian National Petroleum Company (NNPC) Limited has secured a $5 billion loan from AfrieximBank to fund the upstream sector of the country.

Naija News reports that NNPC Group Managing Director, Mele Kyari, made this known on Thursday via his official Twitter handle.

Kyari stated that the deal was an offshoot of the meeting between the NNPC team and the Chairman of the Board of Directors and President of the African Export-Import Bank (Afreximbank), Benedict Oramah, in Cairo, Egypt, on Wednesday.

He disclose that the $5bn loan would be used to support Nigeria’s upstream businesses and facilitate NNPC’s energy supply programme and transition.

The NNPC boss added that the loan would create opportunities for both the national oil firm and the financial institution.
