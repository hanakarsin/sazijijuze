Gov't Targetting 30 Million Doses of Johnson and Johnson Vaccine, CS Kagwe

May 2024 · 2 minute read

- Health CS Mutahi Kagwe said the government was committed to combating the spread of the COVID-19 pandemic

- Kagwe stated the government was working with the African Union (AU) to acquire the Johnson and Johnson vaccines which will be delivered in the country in phases

- On Wednesday, May 12, KEMRI scientists had identified 15 cases of the Indian COVID-19 strain from 17 samples

Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe has said the government is targeting to secure 30 million doses of the Johnson and Johnson vaccine to speed up the vaccination programme against COVID-19.

The CS said the government was working with the African Union (AU) to cover the vaccine for the 60% adult population.

"The governments working with the UN to secure 30 million Doses of Johnson and Johnson vaccines to help stop the spread of COVID-19 in the country and will also supplement AstraZeneca vaccine due to its low supply," Kagwe said.

According to Kagwe, the government's decision to import the Johnson & Johnson vaccines was invoked by challenges in the global supply of the AstraZeneca vaccine due to the situation in India.

"As most of us may be aware, India is currently recording high numbers of COVID-19 related-cases as well as deaths, and the demand for AstraZeneca vaccine is very high, making it impossible for the Covax facility to meet the much-needed demand in Africa," Kagwe stated earlier.

Recently, the country has been recording a surge in cases involving the new Indian variant, which has continued to claim thousands of lives in the South Asian county.

The CS said more variants would continue to emerge and would be reported in the country. He warned that it was upon each citizen to take a personal initiative to protect themselves.

As of Wednesday, May 12, some 15 new cases of the Indian COVID-19 strain were identified from 17 samples tested by the Kenya Medical Research Institute (KEMRI) in Kilifi.
