Senator Mutula Kilonzo pledges to provide legal support if family of ferry victims moves to court

July 2024 Â· 3 minute read

- Kilonzo says the accident scared him since the last time he used the ferry with his family, they did not disembark from their vehicle as required

- He, however, insists that heads must roll and that safety standards at the ferry are raised to the highest standard possible

- Bodies of Kighenda, 35, and four-year-old Amanda were retrieved 13 days after they drowned at the Likoni Channel

- Kalonzo Musyoka and Governor Kivutha Kibwana have called for safety measures to be installed to prevent future accidents

- Last week, President Uhuru Kenyatta started cracking the whip by sending home KFS board members as investigation in the accident continue

Makueni Senator Mutula Kilonzo Jnr has pledged to provide legal services to the family that lost two of their kin in the Likoni ferry accident.

Mariam Kighenda, 35, and four-year-old Amanda Mutheu drowned on the evening of Sunday, September 29, after their vehicle slid from MV Harambee ferry midstream and plunged in the Indian Ocean.

The two were buried at their home in Kilome, Makueni County in a ceremony punctuated by surging calls for prosecution of Kenya Ferry Services (KFS) bosses for negligence.

"I have heard and also read that you intend to file a case against KFS. If you do, count me in your legal team. We must deal with this negligence.This incident really scared me to the core. Personally, I have used the ferry service with my family and the last we crossed the channel aboard it (ferry), we did not alight from our vehicle. We stayed inside," said the youthful senator.

Kilonzo's remarks come hardly after a week after KFS asked widower John Wambua to move to court where he will be able to seek compensation overcompensation.

The service had, however, issued KSh 200,000 which it said was a token to facilitate burial.

Makueni Governor Kivutha Kibwana termed the accident as a wake-up call for the country adding that it was time authorities breathed life in how they responded to emergencies in a move to forestall similar calamities in future.

"Thorough investigations should be done to establish what exactly caused the accident. These should be the last deaths at the ferry," said Kibwana.

Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka, on his part, insisted that there was a need for the ferries to be repaired as their current state risked thousands of lives who use the vessels to cross the channel daily.

Kalonzo nonetheless called for immediate arrest and prosecution of safety officers at KFS.

"It is clear to me that this was a case of criminal negligence. KFS officials should have raised the ramp of the ferry as required. They should be arrested immediately and charged with criminal negligence," he said.

Other who attended the ceremony are Makueni MP Dan Maanzo, Machakos Deputy Governor Adelina Mwau and Kaiti MP Joshua Kimilu.
