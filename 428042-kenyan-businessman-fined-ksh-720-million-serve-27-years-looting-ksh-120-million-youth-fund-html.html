Kenyan Businessman Fined KSh 720 Million or Serve 27 Years for Looting KSh 120 Million from Youth Fu

June 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Ngamau is accused of using his company Quorandum Limited's Chase Bank account to receive money from the Youth Fund
  • The businessman's company was ordered to compensate the Youth Fund Enterprises KSh 180,364,789, which was lost
  • The businessman, alongside other three suspects, was arrested by police in 2016 and arraigned before the Nairobi-Milimani Anti-Corruption Court

A Nairobi court has fined businessman Mukuria Ngamau KSh 720.8 million, or in default, serve 27 years in prison for defrauding the Youth Enterprise Development Fund (YEDF) of KSh 180 million.

Ngamau is accused of using his company Quorandum Limited's Chase Bank account to receive money from the Youth Fund.

Conspiracy to commit an economic crime

Milimani Chief Magistrate Douglas Ogoti found Ngamau guilty of conspiracy to commit an economic crime, unlawful acquisition of public property and falsifying documents.

The businessman's company was ordered to compensate the Youth Fund Enterprises KSh 180,364,789, which was lost.

Ngamau pleaded with the court for a lenient sentencing, saying that he had tested positive for COVID-19 and is the breadwinner of his family.

But in a quick rejoinder, Ogoti dismissed his appeal, ruling that Anti-Corruption and Economic Crimes dictated the sentence.

Citizen TV reports that Eva Kanyuira (for the prosecution) told the court that the accused did not deserve a non-custodial sentence, arguing that his crime was severe if the amount of money looted was anything to go by.

Conspired to transfer funds

Ogoti determined that the former Youth Fund boss, between November 17, 2014, and May 4, 2015, conspired to transfer the funds from the agency to his company.

According to a statement issued by the Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC), the anti-graft agency received information that officials from the Youth Enterprise Development Fund conspired with Ngamau's company to embezzle the sum of money.

''Upon completion of investigations, the EACC submitted its report to the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) who concurred with recommendations to charge Catherine Namuye Akelo (Chief Executive Officer at Youth Enterprise Development Fund, Bruce Odhiambo (Chairman Youth Enterprise Development Fund),'' EACC said.

Both Odhiambo and Namuye are deceased.

The suspects were arrested by police in 2016 and arraigned before the Nairobi-Milimani Anti-Corruption Court, where they all pleaded guilty.

''The first two accused persons, Odhiambo and Namuye passed away during the pendency of the case and the charges against them abated,'' the EACC added.

Hassan Wario's case

On Thursday, September 16, TUKO.co.ke reported that former Sports Cabinet Secretary Hassan Wario was handed six years imprisonment or a fine of KSh 3.6 million over the 2016 Olympic Rio financial misappropriation.

Anti-corruption magistrate Elizabeth Juma convicted Wario of abuse of office in a case in which he was accused of embezzling KSh 55 million meant for Kenyan athletes in the 2016 Olympics games.

The court said Wario took advantage of his office and added three other people in the team Kenya and paid allowances, thus resulting in the loss of public funds since he did not have the mandate to allow the three to travel to Rio.
