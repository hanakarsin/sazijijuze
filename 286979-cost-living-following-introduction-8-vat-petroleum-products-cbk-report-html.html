Cost of living to go up following introduction of 8% VAT on petroleum products - CBK report

- The rate of inflation dropped to 4% in August from 4.4% in July 2018, CBK report showed

- Decrease in food prices was cited as the main reason for the drop in cost of living

- Non-food-non-fuel inflation also remained below 5% during the period under review

- The Central Bank of Kenya noted inflation would rise in near future due to new fuel levy

- The rise would, however, remain within the target range due to lower food prices

A new report by the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) has confirmed the overall cost of living will go up in the near future following introduction of 8% Value Added Tax (VAT) on petroleum products.

The banking sector regulator noted inflation will soar up not just because of the impact of the new fuel levy but also increase in international oil prices.

A statement issued by CBK's Monitory Policy Committee and seen by TUKO.co.ke on Tuesday, September 25, indicated the month-on-month inflation remained within the target range in July and August 2018, largely due to lower food prices.

"The inflation fell to 4% in August from 4.4% in July following deceases in food prices which offset the increase in energy prices, including the increase in the price of charcoal," CBK noted.

According to the regulator, non-food-non-fuel (NFNF) inflation remained below 5%, an indication that demand driven inflationary pressures remained muted within the period under review.

However, Kenyans would soon have to dig deeper into their pockets to put food on their tables or acquire basic products and services.

"Overall inflation is expected to rise in the near term following the implementation of the VAT on petroleum products in September 2018 and its impact on other prices, as well as increases in international oil prices," the regulator noted.

Kenyans had already started feeling the pinch following introduction of 8% VAT on the previously zero-rated petroleum products.

Fuel prices immediately soared up, leading to increase in cost of other basic products.

The inflation rate, CBK clarified, would nevertheless remain within the target range due to lower food prices brought about by favourable weather.
